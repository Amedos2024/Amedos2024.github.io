Solving The Net-Zero-Equation For Norway

I contributed to an in-depth analysis and report titled "Solving The Net-Zero-Equation For Norway". The project focused on outlining Norway's pathway to achieving net-zero emissions by 2050. This involved identifying key technological innovations, necessary economic adjustments, and societal changes that Norway, as one of the world’s largest oil and gas producers, must implement to align with its climate targets.

In my role, I analyzed technological innovations and supply chain requirements, with an emphasis on green technologies such as carbon capture and storage (CCS), hydrogen, and biofuels. Additionally, I examined Norway's advancements in renewable energy, including hydropower, solar, and wind, which place the country at the forefront of Europe's energy transition.

The report highlighted nine critical requirements for achieving net-zero emissions, covering physical infrastructure, capital allocation, societal shifts, and governance. Our recommendations aim to support Norway’s goal of cutting greenhouse gas emissions while maintaining energy security and economic stability.

Case Study – Technical and Economic Study of Oil Field Development

I participated in a detailed technical and economic analysis focused on developing an oil field in a complex geological environment. My role involved assessing key reservoir characteristics, analyzing petrophysical properties, and evaluating both exploration and appraisal well data to optimize recovery.

The study included geological and petrophysical analysis of formations such as the Pampa and Andes, identifying hydrocarbon potential, and conducting fluid dynamics assessments. Using advanced reservoir simulation and financial modeling, we calculated the oil-in-place (OIIP), reserves, and developed a Field Development Plan (FDP). This involved the strategic placement of production and injection wells to maximize recovery while ensuring optimal economic performance.

The economic analysis considered CAPEX, OPEX, and production forecasts over a 15-year horizon. Our recommendations demonstrated that the field was economically viable with a net profit of $319 million and an NPV of $364 per barrel, confirming the project’s financial feasibility.
